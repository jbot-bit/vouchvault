# Spam FAQ

## Overview

This page addresses account limitations resulting from spam reports on Telegram. It explains what happens when accounts are flagged, consequences, and appeals procedures.

## Key Points

**Account Limitations:**
When users report spam, moderators review the messages. Confirmed violations result in temporary account restrictions limiting message-sending capabilities. However, limited accounts retain the ability to message saved contacts and reply to incoming messages.

**Reportable Violations:**
The platform considers unsolicited messages reportable regardless of content type—"a photo, an invite link or a simple 'hello'" can trigger reports if recipients didn't expect them. Users should avoid sending unwanted advertisements, group invitations, random media, and commerce-related content.

**Duration and Escalation:**
First-time violators typically face "a few days" of limitation. Repeated offenses extend restriction periods, potentially resulting in permanent loss of messaging privileges for habitual offenders.

**False Positives:**
New accounts using certain phone numbers may be automatically limited due to "previous owners' activities or...virtual/VOIP numbers." Users believing restrictions were wrongfully applied can contact @SpamBot.

**Appeal Process:**
The platform acknowledges that "even the best systems, algorithms and well-trained people can make mistakes." Users may contact @SpamBot to dispute restrictions, though expedited appeals are not available.
