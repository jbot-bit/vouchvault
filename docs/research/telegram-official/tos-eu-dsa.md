# User Guidance for the EU Digital Services Act

## Overview

Telegram provides this guide to comply with Regulation (EU) 2022/2065, which establishes rules for digital services across the EU. For European users, this guide is part of Telegram's Terms of Service.

## Prohibited Content

Telegram restricts the following on its public platform:

- **Spam**: Unsolicited advertisements and commercial harassment
- **Violence promotion**: Calls for violence, terrorism support, or fundraising for terrorist groups
- **Illegal sexual content**: Child abuse materials, bestiality, and non-consensual intimate images
- **Activities illegal in most countries**: Child abuse, illegal goods/services sales, doxing, and similar violations

## Content Moderation Approach

Telegram employs "a combination of AI-driven and manual moderation for public content, together with processing user reports." The platform prioritizes least restrictive measures while addressing malicious content, with critical restrictions requiring human moderator approval.

## Account Actions

Telegram may:
- Temporarily or permanently suspend account functionality
- Apply FAKE or SCAM labels to impersonation/fraud accounts
- Block or delete accounts, bots, posts, channels, and groups for serious violations

Users receive notification of restrictions and guidance on removal, unless law prevents notification.

## Recommender Systems

Telegram does not use content recommendation algorithms to amplify trending content. The Global Search ranks results by subscriber count and verification status, with regional prioritization. Users can view similar channels based on shared subscribers.

## User Contact Points

- **General users**: Contact [@EURegulation](https://t.me/EURegulation) bot
- **Illegal content reports**: Use https://telegram.org/dsa-report with detailed information

## Regulatory Authority Contacts

EU competent authorities contact Telegram through its European Digital Services Representative (EDSR) at Avenue Huart Hamoir 71, 1030 Brussels, Belgium; dsa.telegram@edsr.eu; or +32 2 216 19 71.

## EU User Scale

As of February 2026, Telegram's online platform services had "significantly fewer than 45 million average monthly active recipients in the EU," remaining below very large platform thresholds.
